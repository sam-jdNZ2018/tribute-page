Bill Gates

A technology pioneer, and one of the men who founded Microsoft

Here is a timeline of some of the most important events in the life of Bill Gates so far. Included are some of the critical events in the history of Microsoft

  • 1955 - Born October 28, in Seattle, Washington to William H. Gates Sr and Mary Maxwell Gates.
  • 1968 - At 13 years of age while attending Lakeside School wrote his first computer program using the language BASIC.
  • 1972 - Formed first business parternship with Paul Allen called Traf-O-Data, whose purpose it was to read raw data from road traffic counters and produce reports for engineers. The partnership wrapped up when the State of Washington began to offer free traffic data processing services.
  • 1973 - Enrolled at Harvard and met Steve Balmer, who would eventually replace Gates as CEO.
  • 1975 - Leaves Harvard and Launches 'Micro-Soft', a business partnership with Paul Allen in Albuquerque, New Mexico. By November 1976, the business was registered simply as Microsoft.
  • 1980 - Released the first version of MS-DOS, an supplemented version of Tim Paterson's 86-DOS.
  • 1985 - First retail version of Microsft Windows launched on November 20.
  • 1990 - First release of Microsoft Office, Office 1.0 on Movember 19.
  • 1994 - Married wife Melinda French on January 1, on the island of Lanai, Hawaii.
  • 1995 - Topped Forbes list of the world's wealthiest people for the first time. Since then has held said position for eighteen of the last twenty-three years until the founder of Amazon, Jeff Bezos, surpassed him in 2017.
  • 2000 - Launched the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, the largest private foundation in the USA.
  • 2002 - Microsoft relases the XBox console and officially joins the console race.
  • 2010 - Signed 'The Giving Pledge' along with Warren Buffet and Joan Salwen. The Giving Pledge is an organisation for whom it's members are set a goal of giving away at least half of the fortune either during their lifetime or at their time of death.
  • 2011 - The Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation launches the 'Reinvent the Toilet' Challenge. The aim is to promote innovation in toilet design to help the 2.5 billion who do not have access to safe and effective sanitation treatment.
  • 2014 - Resigns as Chairman of Microsoft on February 4 to spend more time on his philanthropy.
